Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and has a thick, toxic atmosphere, it is similar in size and structure to Earth but has extremely high surface temperatures. Venus rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets.

Explore Venus on NASA

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only one known to support life, it has a rich atmosphere and liquid water, essential for life as we know it. Earth's magnetic field protects it from harmful solar radiation.

Read about Earth on NASA
Mars

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to the iron oxide (rust) on its surface, it has the largest volcano and canyon in the Solar System. Mars has been a prime target for exploration due to the possibility of past life.

Discover Mars on NASA
Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the Solar System and is mostly made of gas, it has a strong magnetic field and dozens of moons, including the four Galilean moons. The Great Red Spot is a giant storm that has been raging on Jupiter for centuries.

Learn more about Jupiter on NASA
